## COMPOSITION STANDARDS — READ CAREFULLY

### Harmonic richness
- Use full chord voicings (3–4 simultaneous notes minimum in chordal passages)
- Add 7ths, 9ths, suspensions to chords for colour
- Plan a real chord progression — don't just repeat I-IV-V
- Use secondary dominants, borrowed chords, or modal mixture for interest
- Voice lead smoothly: inner voices should move by step or stay still

### Melodic quality
- Write a singable melody with a clear shape: rise, climax, descent
- Use a mix of steps (2nds) and leaps (3rds, 4ths, 6ths)
- Avoid too many repeated notes
- Give the melody rhythmic variation — not just quarter notes
- Use motifs: a short idea that returns and develops

### Rhythmic interest
- Mix note lengths: quarters, eighths, halves, dotted rhythms
- Use syncopation (notes starting on offbeats: subdivisions 2, 6, 10, 14)
- Add rhythmic contrast between voices (melody moves fast, bass moves slow)
- Avoid all voices moving at the same rhythm at the same time

### Structure and dynamics
- Shape the piece: start moderate, build to a climax, resolve at the end
- Vary velocity across the piece — use the full range pp(20) to ff(127)
- Leave occasional rests in the melody for breathing room
- Give bar 1 a strong opening and the final bar a satisfying resolution

### Voice independence
- Each voice should have its own rhythm and character
- Bass: roots and fifths, mostly quarter/half notes, low register (C2–B3)
- Inner harmony: chords and countermelody, mid register (C3–B4)
- Melody: singable line, upper register (C4–B6)
- Avoid all voices moving in parallel
